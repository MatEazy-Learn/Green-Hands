Project Overview

Executive Summary

This project addresses Nyala's critical water access and flood vulnerability challenges. It proposes installing a submersible water pump system to ensure safe water supply while integrating flood-resilient infrastructure. The total cost estimate is $18,000, aligning with Sudanese water project benchmarks.

Background

Nyala, located in South Darfur, faces seasonal flooding due to wadi overflow and inadequate drainage. A 2018 feasibility study highlighted the need for submersible pumps and elevated water storage to mitigate flood risks. This project builds on the Water Supply Extension Project (1986â€“1993) and incorporates modern flood resilience strategies.

Project Objectives

  • Provide reliable water access to 3,000+ residents and students
  • Mitigate flood risks through resilient infrastructure
  • Leverage community-driven approaches (e.g., "Nafeer" system)
  • Ensure cost-effectiveness and long-term sustainability

Project Impact

Clean Water Access

Direct access to clean water for 3,000+ people, reducing waterborne diseases and improving overall health.

Education Benefits

School students will have reliable water access, improving attendance and learning conditions.

Flood Resilience

Flood-resilient infrastructure protects the water supply during seasonal floods, ensuring continuous access.

Technical Feasibility

Proposed System

  • Submersible Pump

    Model SX200-600, selected for durability and low maintenance in flood-prone areas

  • Water Treatment

    Compact filtration unit to ensure potable water

  • Storage

    Elevated tank (10,000-liter capacity) to prevent flood contamination

  • Distribution

    Gravity-fed piped network to schools and communal taps

Flood Resilience Integration

  • Stabilized Soil Blocks (SSBs)

    Used for pump housing and storage tank foundations to withstand flooding

  • Early Warning Systems

    Partner with local authorities to integrate hydro-meteorological monitoring

Community-Driven Approach

Leveraging the traditional "Nafeer" system of communal labor to ensure local ownership and participation.

Sustainability & Risk Management

Sustainability Measures

  • Community Ownership

    Establish a water user association for maintenance

  • Energy Efficiency

    Solar-powered pump option explored in future phases

  • Monitoring

    Biannual performance reviews with UN-Habitat metrics

Risk Assessment

Risk: Flood damage during construction

Mitigation: Use SSBs for temporary flood barriers

Risk: Pump maintenance challenges

Mitigation: Train local technicians; stock spare parts

Risk: Community resistance

Mitigation: Engage "Nafeer" committees for oversight
